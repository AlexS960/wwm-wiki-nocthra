## How Weapons Work

### Weapon Types Determine the Combat Style

Weapon Types determine the basic combat style of your weapon. For example, Swords, Spears, Fans, Umbrellas, and Rope Darts all have different attack ranges, skill styles, and combat roles.

### Martial Arts Change Your Moveset

Martial Arts determine the actual moveset and skills used by a weapon type. Some weapon types have multiple Martial Arts, allowing the same weapon type to fit different playstyles.

For example, Fans can be used for healing and support with Panacea Fan, while Inkwell Fan gives the weapon type a different combat style.

How to Unlock All Martial Arts

### Equip Two Weapons at Once

Players can equip two weapons at the same time and swap between them during combat. This allows you to combine different weapon types, Martial Arts, and roles in one build.

Weapon pairings are important for both PvE and PvP, since some weapons are better for damage, healing, crowd control, survivability, or ranged pressure.

## Weapon Stats Explained

### Damage is Randomly Based on Minimum and Maximum Attack

Your weapon damage is based on the Physical Attack stat range of your weapon. For example, if your weapon has a Physical Attack of 10-16, each hit can randomly apply damage from any number between 10 and 16.

Stats and Attributes Explained

### Can Gain Random Substats

Weapons can gain a random substat starting from Tier 31\. Upon reaching Tier 41, players can tune weapons and add a second substat.

The third substat slot can be obtained at Tier 51, while the last substat slot can be obtained at Tier 56.

### Some Weapons are Best Used as Tuning Materials

Some weapons can gain a **Tuning material substat**, which allows them to be used as tuning materials with an added guarantee to the substat rarity based on the stone icon.

A purple stone icon guarantees a purple or higher substat, while a gold stone icon guarantees a gold substat.

Gear Tuning Guide

### Each Weapon Has Unique Playstyles

Weapons in Where Winds Meet are categorized into various weapon types. Although each weapon type has its own basic combat style, Martial Arts further change how each weapon plays.

Martial Arts can alter a weapon's moveset, skills, role, and preferred combat range, allowing players to create different builds even when using the same weapon type.

Some weapons fall under a specific gear set, which grants bonus damage increases when set conditions are met. These sets offer a smaller bonus when equipped with 2 pieces, but are best worn with 4 pieces to maximize their full effect.

Offensive sets correspond to both weapon slots, as well as the **Disc** and **Pendant** slots. Aligning these 4 equipment slots with the same set should grant you the set bonus.

You can tell that these slots are under the same set if the icons on their upper left are the same.
