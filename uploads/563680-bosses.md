<div class="breadcrumbs-container"><div id="breadcrumbs2"><a href="/Where_Winds_Meet_Wiki" title="Where Winds Meet Wiki">Home</a> &gt; <a href="/World_Information" title="World Information">World Information</a> &gt; <span id="breadcrumbs2-currentitle">Bosses</span></div></div><div class="page-heading-container"><h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading mw-first-heading">Bosses | Where Winds Meet Wiki</h1><div class="page-heading-container-sub"><div class="page-heading-subtitle-container"><div id="contentSub"><div id="mw-content-subtitle"><div class="subpages"></div></div></div></div><div class="page-heading-metadata-container"><div class="article-last-updated">This page was last edited on 28 April 2026, at 18:11.</div><a href="#join-discussion" class="discussion-jump"></a><a href="/index.php?title=Bosses&amp;action=history" class="history-jump" rel="nofollow" data-no-preview="true"></a></div></div></div><div id="bodyContentOuter"><div id="siteSub">From Where Winds Meet Wiki</div><div id="bodyContent" data-mw-ve-target-container=""><div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><p><a href="/Where_Winds_Meet_Wiki" title="Where Winds Meet Wiki">Where Winds Meet</a> offers a wide variety of game modes for its players, and while a lot of these modes lean more into the social and multiplayer aspect of the game, there is also a hefty classical single-player experience to be had. With it, there are bosses to defeat at the end of exploring a particular location, concluding one of the storylines, and even boss-grade enemies that are hanging around in the world.
</p><p>
Some of those aforementioned multiplayer modes may very well also contain bosses for teams of players to defeat collectively, like a classic MMORPG raid, for instance. Note that when encountering bosses as an online group, they have expanded move sets and are much more difficult.<br /></p><h3 class="bonfire" id="Boss_Types">
Boss Types</h3><p>While all of the bosses in <a href="/Where_Winds_Meet_Wiki" title="Where Winds Meet Wiki">Where Winds Meet</a> are considered as Adversaries of Power in the Compendium, they are split into two distinct categories depending on where you encounter them. Not only does the type of boss indicate how you will find and battle them, but it will also tell you if the fight can be repeatedly challenged.
</p>
<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-4"><h4 class="special" id="Campaign_Bosses">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/File:Campaign-challenge-icon-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="campaign challenge icon where winds meet wiki guide" src="https://static0.fextralifeimages.com/file/wherewindsmeet/2/2c/Campaign-challenge-icon-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png" decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="50" height="50" class="mw-file-element" /></a></span> Campaign Bosses</h4>Powerful Adversaries encountered during the Main Story or during Location Campaigns(Side Stories). Once you have conquered these Bosses in their original environments, you can challenge them again as a Campaign Challenge. To reach a Campaign Challenge, go to Main Menu → Wandering Paths → Challenges → Select the Campaign(Boss fight) you want to repeat. From here, the game will switch to the map and show you the location of the Boss together with rewards. Click on the Teleport button in the bottom-right corner. This will teleport you to the arena where you fought a Boss before during the Main Story or a Side Story. To start a Boss fight, simply walk towards the Boss.<br /><br />Some Bosses, like Heartseeker, require an additional step to start the fight. You need to interact with an object that leads to a Boss fight. For Heartseeker, it is the Mysterious Book on the table right in front of the player character.
</div>
<div class="col-sm-4"><h4 class="special" id="World_Bosses">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/File:World-boss.png" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="world boss" src="https://static0.fextralifeimages.com/file/wherewindsmeet/b/bd/World-boss.png" decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="50" height="50" class="mw-file-element" /></a></span> World Bosses</h4>Opponents of great power found lurking in various parts of the open world. The World Bosses can be found in the locations marked on the map, and you must approach them to begin a battle. World Bosses across Qinghe can be weakened using the Bell of Demoncalm [[[Interactive Map|interactive map link]]].
<p>Please note that each World Boss can be defeated <b>only once in the in-game world</b>.
</p>
</div>
<div class="col-sm-4"><h4 class="special" id="Trial_Bosses">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/File:Trial-bosses-bosses-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.webp" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="trial bosses bosses where winds meet wiki guide" src="https://static0.fextralifeimages.com/file/wherewindsmeet/f/fb/Trial-bosses-bosses-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.webp" decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="50" height="50" class="mw-file-element" /></a></span> Trial Bosses</h4>For players who desire more challenge. Trial Bosses are Campaign or World Bosses players defeated before, but with a twist. There's a list of tasks for each Boss fight. Players are required to reach a certain goal within the fight. Doing so yields a set of rewards unattainable elsewhere. Trial Bosses requires a lot of practice, and there's a Leaderboard showing the top 20 players in different categories.
<p>Main Menu → Wandering Paths → Trials → Select the Boss fight.
</p>
</div></div>
<p>In addition to Adversaries of Power, <a href="/Where_Winds_Meet_Wiki" title="Where Winds Meet Wiki">Where Winds Meet</a> also has a set of mini-bosses known as Moderate Talents. These enemies are stronger, more dangerous variants of regular enemies who can usually be found guarding Outposts or other similar areas. While they are not classed as full-fledged bosses, they can be challenging encounters to overcome and should be kept in mind while exploring the game's many regions.<br /></p>
<h3 class="bonfire" id="Boss_Rewards">
Boss Rewards</h3><p>Depending on when you encounter and defeat a boss, you will obtain a different set of rewards. Upon defeating most bosses for the first time in <a href="/Where_Winds_Meet_Wiki" title="Where Winds Meet Wiki">Where Winds Meet</a>, you will be awarded a small handful of materials and valuable items, such as Echo Jade, Martial Arts Tomes, Coins, and Character EXP. These items will be given to you directly after defeating the boss and can only be claimed once per account.
</p>
<p>Meanwhile, if you conquer a boss you've already beaten in a Campaign Challenge or similar environment, they will give you different rewards than they did at the end of your first encounter. These rewards contain several items from specific gear sets as well as Cosmetic Chests containing various new apparel items (both of which differ from boss to boss), as well as other items like currency and EXP. However, unlike your initial clear, players must spend Energy to claim boss rewards from a Campaign Challenge, with each run costing 20 Enmergy.

</p>
<p>Some Boss rewards contain <a href="/Inner_Ways" title="Inner Ways">Inner Way</a> Tomes. These help you unlock an Inner Way in the Inner Way menu.

</p>
<p style="text-align:center"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/File:World-boss-rewards-image-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="world boss rewards image where winds meet wiki guide" src="https://static0.fextralifeimages.com/file/wherewindsmeet/thumb/3/30/World-boss-rewards-image-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png/600px-World-boss-rewards-image-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png" decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="600" height="338" class="mw-file-element" srcset="https://static0.fextralifeimages.com/file/wherewindsmeet/3/30/World-boss-rewards-image-where-winds-meet-wiki-guide.png 1.5x" /></a></span></p>

<h3 class="bonfire" id="Boss_Companion_Assist_and_Assist">
Boss Companion Assist and Assist</h3><p>Players who find Boss fights too challenging can use the Companion Assist and Assist functions. These simplify the combat by calling an AI bot or real player(s) to fight a Boss with you. The Companion Assist option lets you select from a variety of NPCs you've met throughout the <a href="/Walkthrough" title="Walkthrough">Main Story</a>. The available Companion options depend on your Main Story progress. The Assist function lets you invite one or more real-world players into your in-game world to help you fight a Boss. The usefulness of this function entirely depends on player availability and willingness to help.
</p>
<p>To use one of these functions, players need to approach a signpost with the function's name and use it. These posts are placed right before the boss encounter, no matter if it is a World or Campaign Boss. Sometimes players need to backtrack a bit to bump into such posts. The Companion Assist and Assist signposts are disabled for Trial Bosses.

</p>
